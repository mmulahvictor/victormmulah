📅 Born February 8 · Kenyan

Victor Mmulah is a full-stack software developer and ICT Officer from western Kenya — a region defined by the shimmer of Lake Victoria, red laterite roads, and communities building futures with what they have.

His path into software was not a straight line. It ran through hardware repairs at KALRO, an internship at INNODEMS where a single coding sprint reoriented his career, a gruelling 7-month bootcamp at Moringa School, and a 4-week piscine at Zone01 Kisumu — all while holding down a full-time job.

Today he works at Edutab Africa, building the GoLearn platform — developing Cybersecurity and African Children’s Stories courses, integrating Moodle with OnlyOffice, and contributing to R-Instat open-source tools used across Africa.

He writes so anyone — child, teen, or adult — can understand technology. He plays chess and Euro Truck Simulator 2 with the same long-haul patience he brings to debugging production systems.

History
The Historian

Victor writes about the people western Kenya chose to forget. His piece on Moraa wa Ng’iti — a 19th-century Gusii prophetess who foretold British colonization, survived torture, led resistance, and died peacefully at 100+ — is not a tech article. It is an act of cultural memory.

“She was tortured for what she knew. She was honoured by the First Lady in 2017. History is not always late.”

That a software developer chooses to write about anti-colonial heroines from his backyard says everything about who he is beyond the terminal.

Character

What makes Victor, Victor

These are not buzzwords. They are patterns that emerge consistently across his projects, questions, writing, and choices.

01Systems Thinker

Victor consistently asks about integration, workflows, and architecture. Moodle + OnlyOffice. React + Rails. Flutter + offline storage. He thinks in systems. The same patience that makes him a long-haul trucker in Euro Truck Simulator 2 makes him an infrastructure architect in real life.

02Practical Engineer

“How do I integrate X?” “What is the best configuration for Y?” His questions are rarely abstract. He learns by building, not theorising. Every training environment he chose — Moringa, Zone01, INNODEMS — was hands-on and implementation-focused.

03Generalist Curiosity

Software engineering. Investment strategy. Kenyan colonial history. Gaming simulation. Cycling data. Chess. Podcasts. The breadth of Victor’s interests is not scattered — it reflects a mind that finds patterns everywhere. His EdTech work is better because he understands storytelling.

04Builder Mentality

Victor does not just consume — he creates. Flutter apps. Moodle integrations. Scratch curricula. Medium articles. Cycling journals. Historical essays. His writing philosophy — simple enough for a child, deep enough for a developer — is itself a communication system he built deliberately.
